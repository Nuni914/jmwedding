Our Story

Joshua Brown

I was born in Arlington, Texas, and grew up moving often because my dad worked for Boeing. While it meant starting over in new homes and schools, those moves taught me adaptability, resilience, and how to connect with people from all walks of life. By the time I graduated from high school, I had lived in Texas, Washington, Alabama, and Utah and traveled through more than half of the 50 states. After all those moves, Washington became home, and I’ve now lived here for more than 20 years. I’ve worked in retail since I was a junior in high school, building a career with Rite Aid before joining Walgreens. During my time with Walgreens, I was fortunate to travel to the East Coast four different times on special assignments. Those experiences challenged me, helped me grow as a leader, and ultimately led me to meeting a mentor who encouraged and prepared me for a promotion to Emerging Store Manager. Not long after that promotion, I made what seemed like an ordinary phone call. At the time, I had no idea it would become one of the most important moments of my life. It’s amazing how one small decision can change everything. That single phone call introduced me to an incredibly strong, compassionate, and loving woman—and to her three amazing children. What started as one conversation became a relationship filled with love, laughter, and purpose. Together, we’ve built a family founded on faith, resilience, and unwavering support for one another. As we begin this next chapter together as husband and wife, we’re excited for all the adventures, memories, and blessings that lie ahead. We can’t wait to continue building our life together, surrounded by the love of our family and friends.

Born in San Juan, Puerto Rico on the 14th of September, 1991. Was raised there until the age of 8 where we moved to Bayonne, NJ on the 11th of January, 2000. I was the 7th of 8 kids, the prior 6 are from dad's side (he was a busy bunny) who were already mostly married & with kids, & when the time came, I was the only one he raised since my baby brother died after 5 months of God letting us have him. I was raised in Bayonne, I went through a lot of ordeals but one of the greastest changes I had was being able to meet some amazing people- Lina, Gelica, & Paige. This happened in 5th grade & I never realized how crucial that meeting would be for my future. Like everyone else, I went through a lot of horrible things but in the midst of that I became a mom to 3 beautiful kids- Jason, Victor, & Eden. Raising kids alone is very difficult, obviously giving birth doesn't come with a "How To" book with the birth certificate & social but I've managed to do well with what I knew & with what I had. For those that know our story, they can say that I will always put my kids first. If it means sacrificing myself for the good of them, I wouldn't hesitate or think about it. It's how it led me to being in Virginia, I was told to choose between homelessness or giving Jason a roof over his head & ofcourse I chose Jason. Thanks to this decision, it led me to eventually move out here to the West, not only to keep hiding from those that aren't safe people but to also be able to breath & start over even if we didn't have anybody. But who would've thunk that seeing a job opportunity online & doing a simple click would lead to the phone call that changed my life forever. Because at the other end of the phone was Josh asking to set-up an interview time so I could go in. I got the job & we worked in the same store for a year. No, we didn't work together, it was just seeing each other in passing. But toward the end of our time in the same store, we got to work together during the closing shift & I got to see how he truly was. This was even a bigger change we never thought we would have...all because of a click of a button. My kids now have an amazing father & I found someone that not only completes me but is kind & understanding & more than willing to learn how to better help me while listening to me. He's the greatest person I have ever met. Atleast Walgreens did something right!

Marlene Torres Santiago
